# Design Principles

## Reduce cognitive load
Keep screens focused and language plain. Remove non-essential decisions from core workflows.

## Surface the next action
Every major screen should make the immediate next step obvious for students and teachers.

## Prefer guided simplicity over flexibility
Choose strong defaults and clear pathways over highly configurable flows that increase confusion.

## Support executive function
Use structure, sequencing, and visual progress cues to support planning, initiation, and completion.

## Preserve teacher coaching over policing
Teacher-facing views should reveal where support is needed, not maximize monitoring pressure.

## Keep reflection lightweight
Reflection prompts should be brief, structured, and tied to meaningful workflow transitions.

## Make peer critique structured and emotionally safe
Use clear roles, prompts, and expectations so critique improves work quality without social harm.

## Respect classroom device realities
Design for mixed device quality, constrained time windows, and variable network conditions.
