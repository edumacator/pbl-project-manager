# Pedagogy Rules

## Educational assumptions
- Adolescent executive function is still developing.
- Students commonly struggle with task initiation, decomposition, prioritization, and long-horizon planning.
- Long projects often create overwhelm and avoidance.
- Students benefit from visible structure and near-term steps.
- Peer critique needs scaffolding to be useful and emotionally safe.
- Reflection works best when embedded in regular workflow transitions.

## Product implications
- Break large actions into smaller, visible steps.
- Reduce interface noise and avoid decision overload.
- Prefer one clear next step over many competing choices.
- Use milestone-based structure for longer work.
- Embed reflection prompts at transition points.
- Scaffold collaboration with clear roles and expectations.

## AI use rules
- AI should support decomposition, planning, and metacognition.
- AI should not remove productive thinking from students.
- AI should guide student workflow rather than dominate it.
