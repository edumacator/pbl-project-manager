# Pedagogy Rules

Our product decisions are grounded in the following educational assumptions.

## Educational Assumptions

- **Adolescent Development**: Executive function (planning, initiation, prioritization) is still developing in high schoolers.
- **Overwhelm**: Long-horizon projects without structure lead to student disengagement and anxiety.
- **Metacognition**: Students learn more when they reflect on *how* they are working, not just *what* they are producing.
- **Safe Feedback**: Peer review is a critical skill but requires high levels of scaffolding to be emotionally safe and productive.
- **Initiation**: The hardest part of a project is often knowing where to start.

## Product Implications

- **Break it Down**: The UI should encourage breaking large goals into small, visible steps.
- **Reduce Noise**: Clear out non-essential info that distracts from the current task.
- **Milestone-Based**: Use a clear structure for long-term work.
- **Embedded Reflection**: Ask for reflections at transition points (e.g., when a task is moved to 'Done' or a milestone is reached).
- **Scaffold Collaboration**: Provide sentence starters or clear criteria for peer critique.

## AI Use Rules in Education

- **Scaffold, Don't Replace**: AI should help a student decompose a problem, but the student should make the final decision.
- **Guide the Workflow**: AI should help a student identify the "next step" rather than just giving the answer.
- **Productive Thinking**: AI must not remove the struggle that is necessary for learning. It should provide "just-in-time" support.
