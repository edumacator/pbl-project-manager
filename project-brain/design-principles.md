# Design Principles

## Reduce Cognitive Load
Minimize choices. Surface what matters now. Use "progressive disclosure" to hide advanced settings until needed.

## Surface the Next Action
The interface should guide the user toward the "next step" in their PBL journey, reducing the friction of task initiation.

## Guided Simplicity Over Flexibility
Constraint is a feature. Provide clear pathways instead of infinite configuration options that lead to decision fatigue.

## Support Executive Function
Many students struggle with decomposition and prioritization. The UI should help them break large projects into manageable steps.

## Teacher Coaching Over Policing
Design for visibility that empowers conversation. Show *why* a team is stuck so a teacher can intervene productively.

## Lightweight Reflection
Reflection should feel like a natural part of the "doing," not an extra chore at the end. Use short, structured prompts.

## Safe Peer Critique
Scaffold feedback loops so they are structured, objective, and emotionally safe for adolescents.

## Device Realities
Ensure the platform is responsive and works well on Chromebooks and mobile devices common in high school classrooms.
