# Feature Guardrails

To preserve product focus, avoid drifting into these unintended product categories.

## Do Not Accidentally Turn This Into:

### A Generic Workplace PM Suite
Avoid: Billable hours, resource forecasting, Jira-level complexity.
Instead: Keep tasks and milestones lightweight and project-based.

### An LMS Clone
Avoid: Gradebook focus, comprehensive content hosting, assignment submission silos.
Instead: Orchestrate the *workflow* of the project; let the LMS handle the grading.

### A Teacher Surveillance System
Avoid: Logging idle time, tracking individual mouse movements, policing minute-by-minute activity.
Instead: Show team velocity, "stuck" signals, and quality of reflection.

### AI-Replacing-Student-Thinking
Avoid: "Click a button to generate a whole project," "AI finishes the task for the student."
Instead: AI scaffolds decomposition, prompts reflection, and helps with metacognition.

### Over-Configurable Admin Platforms
Avoid: Deeply nested setting menus, infinite custom fields, "enterprise" permission matrices.
Instead: Smart defaults that work for 90% of classrooms.

## Guardrails

- **Student workflows stay simple**: If it takes more than 3 clicks to do a core action, rethink it.
- **Avoid duplicate systems**: Do not add parallel task systems for different "types" of work.
- **AI as a Guide**: AI should prompt the student to think, not do the thinking for them.
- **Teacher as Coach**: Tools should focus on orchestration and intervention visibility.
